Chapter One

Where it began — corridors of GIET

We met in college. Not dramatically — just the way real things begin. Quietly. In the same corridors, the same shared chaos, the same batch of friends that somehow pushed us into each other's orbit.

Gopi was there too — our common thread. The three of us, woven together before any of us knew what that meant.

Chapter Two

The bike ride — Pattiseema & Korukonda

Korukonda — the whole batch went. Loud, crowded, beautiful. But Pattiseema was different. That was just for you. After a fight, before night, I rode there to find you. A surprise that said everything I couldn't.

The Godavari didn't need us to say anything. It already knew.

Chapter Three

The window — RAJAMUNDRY TO ELURU

I used to drop you from college to the bus stand. A small thing. But every time the bus would pull away, I'd look up — and there you were at the window, looking down.

In that one moment between the bus moving and you disappearing — I think I fell in love with you. Quietly. Without knowing.

"That feeling of missing someone who hasn't even left yet."

Chapter Four

Four cities. One thread that never snapped.

Rajamundry to Mysore. Mysore to Hyderabad. And me, in Lucknow — wondering every day how distance became just a word we refused to let mean anything.

Long distance didn't break us. Our own silence tried harder than any map ever did.

The hardest chapter

Some walls are real.

Families. Ages. Castes. We didn't choose these walls — but we know they exist. We've looked at each other through them more times than I can count, wondering what the other side looks like.

I won't pretend I have answers. I don't. I only know what I feel — and what I feel has never once changed, no matter how loud the world got around us.

You once said we can't do anything, only hope. So let this website be that hope. Written down. Made real. Sent to you because you deserved to know — even if we never get to say it out loud anywhere else.
